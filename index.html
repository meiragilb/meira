Hi! I'm Meira, a PhD Student at the University of Washington's Information School studying digital privacy.

I am interested in how technology, particularly social media platforms, fosters new forms of interpersonal, “social” surveillance between peers. Specifically, I am interested in questions such as:

  • How and why do people monitor, surveil, and disclose information about others?
  • What are the risks of these practices?

To address the first question, I use qualitative HCI methods to analyze user behaviors, perceptions, and reactions to social surveillance practices. To address the second, I rely on work from usable privacy & security to conduct risk assessments of these technologies, and surveillance studies to critically address how these practices engage with identity and power.

Before I started my PhD, I worked on Meta's privacy incident management team and recieved my Bachelor's in Information Science from Cornell University.

When I'm not researching privacy, I love to read, spend time outside, and hang out with my two cats, Scallion and Pancake.
